Cow

Cows are domesticated ungulates that are raised for their milk, meat, and hides. They are known for their gentle nature and are often seen grazing in pastures.

Cows are social animals that live in herds and have a complex social structure. They communicate with each other through vocalizations, body language, and even facial expressions.

Cows are herbivores, primarily feeding on grass and other vegetation. They have a unique digestive system with four stomach compartments that allow them to efficiently break down tough plant material.

Cows play a significant role in agriculture, providing milk, meat, and leather products. They are also used in some cultures for religious and ceremonial purposes.
